Piano

You should assume that taking piano lessons will be fun and challenging. Your teacher’s responsibility is to see that lessons are enjoyable and productive. Some students learn best with a defined course of study; others may thrive in a less structured environment. My teaching is personalized to each student.

Read More

I’ve found it best to begin a new student with a six-month trial period. During that period I’ll draw on my many years of experience to determine whether the student is a good match for my teaching style.

Here are several qualities I look for:

  • The student loves music and wants to make music.
  • The student takes direction well.
  • The student commits to a practice routine and comes to lessons prepared.
  • The student will have access to an acoustic piano that is in tune and well regulated or to an electric keyboard with a minimum of 76 keys and a weighted action (i.e. keys that offer resistance, like a common piano, not like the keys of an organ.)


I teach so that my students can read music, count rhythms, practice productively, and play with feeling and confidence. Eventually, students will be able to pick up a piece of music and know how to learn it themselves.
